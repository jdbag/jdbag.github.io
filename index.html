✦ Vocabulary Building Tips

📖 Read Every Day

Reading books, articles, and news is the most natural way to build vocabulary. When you encounter an unfamiliar word, look it up immediately and add it to your Favorites for review later.

🗂️ Use Flashcards

Write new words on flashcards — word on one side, definition on the other. Review them daily. Spaced repetition is scientifically proven to improve long-term memory retention.

🗣️ Use Words in Context

The best way to remember a word is to use it in sentences. Try to write three different sentences with each new word you learn. This builds meaningful mental connections.

🔤 Learn Word Roots

Many English words share Latin and Greek roots. Learning roots like "bene" (good), "port" (carry), or "dict" (say) helps you guess the meaning of unfamiliar words instantly.

🎯 Focus on Context Clues

Pay attention to how words are used in sentences. The surrounding words often reveal the meaning. This skill helps you read faster and understand more without stopping.

🌐 Try Multiple Languages

Learning a second language expands your understanding of your first language. Use our Translate feature to explore how concepts differ across Arabic, French, Spanish, and more.

✦ Frequently Asked Questions
What is Your Dictionary?
Your Dictionary is a free online English dictionary that provides instant definitions, synonyms, example sentences, and audio pronunciations. It also includes a translation tool for 30+ languages, a vocabulary quiz, and the ability to save favorite words for later study — all without any registration.
Is Your Dictionary completely free?
Yes, Your Dictionary is 100% free. There is no registration, subscription, or payment required. All features including word search, translation, pronunciation, quiz, and favorites are available at no cost to every visitor.
How many languages does the translator support?
The translation feature supports over 30 languages including English, Arabic, French, Spanish, German, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Turkish, Hindi, Persian, Indonesian, Vietnamese, Ukrainian, Greek, Hebrew, and many more. You can translate in both directions between any two supported languages.
How does the pronunciation feature work?
Your Dictionary uses your browser's built-in text-to-speech (TTS) technology. This works in all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari. Click the speaker icon on any word result to hear it pronounced. The translation page also lets you hear both the original text and the translated output.
Is my data and search history private?
Your favorites, search history, and quiz scores are stored locally in your browser only. This data never leaves your device and cannot be accessed by any server or third party. You can delete it at any time through your browser settings. We do not collect personal information.
What API powers the word definitions?
Definitions are sourced from the Free Dictionary API at dictionaryapi.dev, an open-source and publicly available service. Translations are powered by the MyMemory API. Both are free services that do not require authentication.
Can I use Your Dictionary on my phone?
Yes, Your Dictionary is fully responsive and optimized for mobile phones and tablets. The interface adapts to any screen size, so you get the same full experience whether you are on a desktop, laptop, tablet, or smartphone.
